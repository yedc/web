What makes people unhappy?

Nowadays, 4 out of 15 people experience mental disorders or affected by depression. Suicide occurs in people of all ages, including children, but adults in middle- and late-middle age have the highest suicide rate. As an extreme action caused by mental suffering it is often overlooked and not been talked about. Therefore, I choose this topic for my project and try to find out what factor may affect the suicide rate.

By comparing UK suicide data with a few other figures, such as average income, population, household income and so on, I found that there are similar patterns in the UK unemployment rate and suicide rate.

Annual Rates Comparison, UK (2000 - 2014)

I looked at UK's unemployment rate from year 2000 to 2014, and the suicide rate in the same period, they seem to have no correlations at first sight, but the trends become very similar when I move the unemployment rate chart to the right. There seems to be a 2 years' gap for the patterns to match.

 

Annual Number Comparison, England (1998 - 2008 vs 2000 - 2010)

Then I try to compare the rates cover a longer period.

I look into England's unemployment number and suicide number, and compare them with a 2 years' time difference.

The upper chart is the unemployment numbers from year 1998 to 2008.

Conclusion

Although the unemployment situation might somehow affect the suicide rate, there is still little evidence to explain why some people attempt suicide. Research has shown many people who die by suicide have a mental illness, most commonly depression or an alcohol problem. Employment may just be one of the reasons.

* If you are concerned that you are developing a mental health problem you should seek the advice and support of your GP as a matter of priority. If you are in distress and need immediate help and are unable to see a GP, you should visit your local A&E. There is also help and support available from Mind. Sharing a problem is often the first step to recovery.
